TrustOS answers one question for your business: *can you trust this person, credential, or claim — under your policy, right now?* Whether you're a lender verifying income, an employer checking qualifications, or a government service confirming identity, TrustOS runs the same privacy-preserving verification pipeline with only the policy configuration changing between use cases.

## What TrustOS does

TrustOS sits between parties that need to make a trust decision (verifiers) and parties that hold cryptographic proof of facts about themselves (holders). It validates credentials, checks revocation status, enforces your policy, runs optional confidential compute models, and returns a structured result — without retaining raw claim data.

## Get started in four steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register as a verifier">
    Register your organization on the Trust Registry. You'll receive API credentials (mTLS certificate and OAuth client) to authenticate your requests.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a policy">
    Define which credential types, assurance levels, and claims your verification flow requires. Call `POST /policies` or use the Marketplace to seed from a template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a verification">
    Call `POST /verifications` with your `policyId` and channel preference. TrustOS returns a presentation request your user completes in their wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive the result">
    Get `Approve`, `Decline`, or `Review` with risk and trust scores via webhook or polling. The result never contains raw credential data.
  </Step>
</Steps>
