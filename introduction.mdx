TrustOS is a standards-based verification platform that answers one question with a structured, auditable result: can you trust this person, business, or credential — right now, under your policy? As a verifier, you integrate once against the TrustOS API, define policies that describe what you need to know, and receive clean `Approve`, `Decline`, or `Review` decisions along with a risk score and trust score. Raw credential claims never leave the holder's wallet and are never stored or forwarded — your application sees only the outcome.

## How It Works

When you start a verification, TrustOS generates a presentation request and delivers it to the holder's wallet via a QR code, deep link, or USSD prompt. The holder reviews exactly which claims are being requested, consents, and sends a cryptographic proof back to TrustOS. TrustOS validates the proof against the issuer's signature, checks the issuer's registration and trust score, confirms the credential hasn't been revoked, evaluates your policy, and runs any fraud-graph signals — then returns a single structured result to you. The whole validate-and-decide pipeline runs in memory; when the event completes, the raw claims are discarded.

## The Three Actors

Every verification involves three parties working together.

| Actor | Role |
|---|---|
| **Holder** | The person or entity who owns credentials in a digital wallet. The holder controls exactly what is shared and must consent to each verification request before any data leaves their device. |
| **Issuer** | An authority — a bank, employer, regulator, or government agency — that signs and issues credentials to holders. Only issuers registered in the Trust Registry can participate. |
| **Verifier** | You. Your organization calls the TrustOS API to request a verification, define the policy it must satisfy, and receive the structured decision. |

## The Verification Result

Every completed verification returns one of three outcomes along with supporting scores and signals.

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `outcome` | `Approve`, `Decline`, or `Review` — the decision your policy produced. |
| `riskScore` | A float from `0.0` (no risk signals) to `1.0` (high risk) drawn from the fraud graph and validation pipeline. |
| `trustScore` | An integer from `0` to `100` reflecting how strongly you can trust the issuer and the credential chain. |
| `riskFlags` | A list of named signals that contributed to the risk score, such as known fraud patterns or anomalous presentation behavior. |
| `proxy` | A pseudonymous, stable identifier for the holder within your scheme — lets you recognise a returning user across sessions without storing any PII. |
| `reasons` | Human-readable explanation of the decision, suitable for case review. |

<Note>
  **Privacy-first by design.** Raw credential claim values exist only in memory during a single verification event and are discarded the moment it completes. TrustOS never writes raw claims to any durable store — not a database, not a log. What reaches your application is the decision, scores, and a pseudonymous proxy identifier. This is not a configuration option; it is enforced in the platform's code and audit schema.
</Note>
